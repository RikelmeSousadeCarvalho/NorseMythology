Ragnarök

Ragnarök is the term used to refer to the succession of catastrophic events that are part of the eschatology (theory about the end of the world) of the ancient Norse, that is, they were supposedly how the Vikings believed that the universe would have its end. During In Ragnarök, according to this view, great battles would take place between the gods and the destruction of the universe would be led by Loki, the sons of Loki and the fire giant named Surtur. The events of Ragnarök would be preceded by the fimbulvetr, a long cycle of three consecutive winters in which there would be terrible frosts, and in which violence and chaos would spread across the world. During the chaos of Ragnarök, the sun and moon, eternally pursued by the wolves Skoll and Hati, would be overtaken and devoured by another wolf called Managarm. After that, the sons of Loki would march over Midgard (world of men) and fight battle with the gods. The sons of Loki were the wolf Fenrir, the serpent of the world called Jörmungandr, and the goddess of the underworld Hel. All were sons of Loki with a giantess (Jotun, in Norse) called Angrboda.

With this thawing, the first god in Norse mythology emerged: Búri. Búri was the father of Borr, and he had 3 sons, whose names are: Vili, Vé and Odin. After a while, the three sons of Borr made a battle against Ymir to finish off the giants. They managed to win and with it came all things in the world. With the bones of Ymir the mountains were created, with the blood the oceans and the rivers were formed, with his teeth the rocks were formed, with his brain the clouds were created and from his hair, the trees and all the vegetation appeared.
