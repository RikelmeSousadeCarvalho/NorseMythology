Worlds

Norse mythology believes in the existence of nine worlds: Álfheim, Asgard, Jotunheim, Midgard, Muspelheim, Nidavellir, Niflheim, Svartalfheim and Vanaheim.

- Álfheim: world of the elves;

- Asgard: world of the gods;

- Jotunheim: world of giants;

- Midgard: world of humans (corresponds to Planet Earth);

- Muspelheim: world of the fire giants;

- Nidavellir: world of dwarves;

- Niflheim: world of dwarves and frost giants;

- Svartalfheim: world of the subterranean gods;

- Vanaheim: world of the Vanir gods.

In addition to the worlds mentioned above, there are mystical places such as Bifrost, it is the bridge responsible for connecting Asgard and Midgard, also called the rainbow bridge, and Valhalla, is the residence of the gods who died in battle, also called Hall of the Dead. This hall was in Asgard and under Odin's domain. The Valkyries were responsible for taking the people that were selected to Valhalla.
