Mímir

Mímir, a significant deity in Norse mythology, stands as the guardian of wisdom and knowledge. Known for his profound insight and counsel, he is often depicted as a decapitated head that Odin, the All-Father, keeps by his side. Mímir's head is preserved and animated through magical means, granting Odin access to invaluable guidance and advice from the past. The well of wisdom, Mímir's source of knowledge, resides beneath the roots of Yggdrasil, the world tree. Odin is said to have sacrificed an eye to drink from this well in exchange for wisdom. Mímir's role underscores the importance of foresight, sagacity, and the pursuit of enlightenment in the intricate tapestry of Norse mythology.
