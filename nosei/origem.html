Origin

The Norse Mythology originated in the Nordic countries, also known as Scandinavians, and originated in Iceland. She tells that everything started with the division of two Kingdoms: Muspenhin, the Kingdom of fire and Nilfihin, the Kingdom of ice. The latter had a division, whose name is Ganagagap. In this division, the flames met the ice, and thus two beings appeared: Ymir, the hermaphrodite god, and also a giant cow called Audhumla. This cow was feeding Ymir, and in order to feed itself, it licked the ice of the kingdom, taking advantage of the water and salt that were present there. In this process, where Audhumla licked the ice, during the 1st day she thawed a man's hair. On the 2nd day, she thawed a man's body and on the third day, she thawed this man's head. With this thawing, the first god in Norse mythology emerged: Búri.

Ymir

Búri was the father of Borr, and he had 3 sons, whose names are: Vili, Vé and Odin. After a while, the three sons of Borr made a battle against Ymir to finish off the giants. They managed to win and with it came all things in the world. With the bones of Ymir the mountains were created, with the blood the oceans and the rivers were formed, with his teeth the rocks were formed, with his brain the clouds were created and from his hair, the trees and all the vegetation appeared.
